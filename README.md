# DW-and-DSS-for-Travel-Demand-Predicttion
NYC Taxi Trip Data Warehouse and Decision Support System for Travel Demand Prediction
---

### **1. Project Overview**
This project aims to design and implement an end-to-end Data Warehouse and a Decision Support System (DSS) tailored for analyzing and predicting travel demand in complex urban environments. By engineering a robust data pipeline, the project transforms raw, large-scale trip records into a structured analytical environment. Subsequently, this Data Warehouse serves as the foundation for training Machine Learning models to forecast future taxi demand across different spatial zones and timeframes, ultimately providing actionable insights through interactive dashboards.

### **2. The Business Problem**
In densely populated metropolitan areas, the imbalance between mobility supply (taxis/ride-sharing vehicles) and passenger demand introduces significant operational inefficiencies:
* **For Drivers & Fleet Operators:** Without predictive insights, drivers often rely on intuition to find passengers, leading to increased "deadheading" (driving without a fare). This wastes fuel, reduces daily revenue, and accelerates vehicle wear and tear.
* **For Passengers:** Demand surges in specific zones during peak hours or special events often result in a shortage of available vehicles, leading to increased wait times and poor customer experience.
* **For Urban Management:** Unoptimized routing and unnecessary cruising contribute heavily to urban traffic congestion and increased carbon emissions.

Addressing these issues requires a transition from reactive dispatching to a **proactive, data-driven Decision Support System** that can accurately forecast *where* and *when* demand will occur.

### **3. Rationale & Choice of the NYC TLC Dataset**
To effectively solve this business problem and demonstrate advanced Data Engineering capabilities, the **New York City Taxi and Limousine Commission (NYC TLC) Trip Record Dataset** was selected as the foundational data source. The rationale for this choice is driven by several key factors:

* **High Practical Value & Industry Benchmark:** The NYC TLC dataset is widely recognized in the data ecosystem as the "gold standard" for spatio-temporal analysis. Working with this dataset effectively simulates a real-world enterprise scenario, requiring the implementation of a modern data stack (ETL pipelines, dimensional modeling, and MLOps).
* **Rich Data Volume & Engineering Complexity:** Dealing with continuous, multi-month data encompassing millions of trips (often spanning multiple gigabytes of compressed Parquet files) presents a genuine Big Data challenge. It necessitates advanced engineering techniques—such as out-of-core processing, lazy evaluation, and efficient partitioning—to process the data without exceeding memory constraints.
* **Granular Spatio-Temporal Features:** The dataset provides highly detailed attributes essential for demand prediction, including precise pickup/drop-off timestamps, trip distances, and specific Location IDs. When integrated with spatial lookup tables, it allows for highly granular time-series forecasting at the borough and neighborhood levels.
* **Direct Applications in Urban Traffic Management:** The insights derived from this dataset extend beyond simple revenue optimization. The predictive models and heatmaps generated by the DSS can be utilized for dynamic pricing strategies, optimal fleet repositioning, and assisting city planners in understanding macro-level traffic flows and transit bottlenecks.
